Snowmobile Design

About

This project aimed to design and animate a fully operational snowmobile blade structure, leveraging SOLIDWORKS to achieve a synthesis of precision engineering and dynamic functionality tailored for extreme winter environments.

Task/Action

  • 3D Modeling: Designed over 100 custom components in SOLIDWORKS, including the blade, mounting brackets, and support structures, ensuring seamless assembly across all subsystems.
  • Developed over 100 precision components using SOLIDWORKS, producing detailed 3D models and 2D technical drawings with exacting dimensioning resulting in seamless integration.
  • Cost Optimization: Adjusted part geometries and material usage, reducing manufacturing costs by 10% without compromising performance.
  • Fatigue Analysis: Simulated cyclic loading conditions to assess the design's durability and predict long-term performance under repeated stress.
  • Material Selection: 6061-T6 aluminum for structural components, balancing strength, machinability, and corrosion resistance for winter environments.
  • Load Path Visualization: Applied SOLIDWORKS SimulationXpress to visualize load paths and identify potential stress concentrations within the blade structure.
  • Design for Manufacturability (DFM): Used SOLIDWORKS DFMXpress to identify potential manufacturing challenges and optimize part design for CNC machining:
  • Standardized hole sizes to accommodate common drill bit diameters.
  • Reduced unnecessary tight tolerances on non-critical surfaces.
  • Simplified complex geometries to streamline machining operations.
  • Evaluated multifaceted impacts safety compliance (ASME standards), environmental sustainability (recyclable alloys), and legal feasibility to deliver a deployable solution.

    • Result

    Produced a blade system with 99.7% dimensional accuracy, validated through animation and physical testing, establishing a benchmark for reliability and performance in snowmobile engineering.
