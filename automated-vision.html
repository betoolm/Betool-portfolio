Automated Vision & Verification Sortation System

About

The Portable Vision System was developed to enhance manual letter mail (LM) and packet sorting efficiency at Canada Post, addressing key operational challenges such as reducing knowledge sort dependency, improving processing performance (PPPH), minimizing missorts, and increasing accessibility for visually impaired employees. Given that manual letter mail comprises only 5-10% of total mail volume but accounts for 35-40% of used sorting hours, the system was designed to streamline workflows, reduce sorting errors, and improve overall service levels. Additionally, budget and market analysis were conducted to assess the project's feasibility and cost effectiveness.

How?

Hardware & System Integration:

  • Designed a barcode scanning system to identify case/workcenter schematics and upload sorting configurations dynamically.
  • Developed a hopper system to present letter mail (LM) and packets to the vision system for automated destination sorting.
  • Integrated OCR (Optical Character Recognition) technology to read handwritten addresses, reducing the reliance on manual interpretation.

Vision System & Automation:

  • Implemented a real-time AI-powered vision system to identify, classify, and route mail items to the correct case slots or mono packet destinations.
  • Designed an automated volume capture system to track and optimize packet & LM counts, contributing to improved operational strategy and forecasting.

Operational & Economic Analysis:

  • Conducted a cost-benefit analysis, demonstrating how automation reduces double handling, sorting time, and labor costs at depots.
  • Performed a market study to assess scalability and adoption feasibility across different Canada Post facilities.
  • Evaluated the impact on service quality, processing speed, and error reduction using performance metrics such as PPPH (pieces per person per hour) and sorting accuracy rates.

Results

The Portable Vision System significantly improved sorting speed, accuracy, and accessibility, leading to tangible benefits for Canada Post, depot operations, and end users:

For Canada Post:

  • Reduced reliance on knowledge sort, minimizing the need for highly trained sorters.
  • 0Improved packet & LM volume tracking through integrated volume capture.
  • Increased PPPH (pieces per person per hour) and service efficiency, reducing operational bottlenecks.
  • Minimized missorts, decreasing processing errors and delivery delays.
  • Enhanced workplace accessibility, ensuring visually impaired employees could actively participate in sorting operations.

For Depot Operations:

  • Higher processing efficiency allowed for more splits and reduced sorting workload at depots.
  • Reduced double handling, cutting down on redundant sorting processes.

For End Users (Customers):

  • Faster and more reliable mail processing led to improved service levels and fewer delivery delays.
  • Cost reductions in operational sorting resulted in greater efficiency and sustainability.

This innovation won the Best Innovation Project Award at Canada Post, demonstrating its significant impact on automation, efficiency, and accessibility within the postal system. Future recommendations include scaling deployment to additional processing centers, refining OCR accuracy, and integrating AI-driven predictive sorting to further enhance efficiency.
