C-Wrist

About

The C-Wrist is a wearable wrist device designed to monitor vital signs for mild COVID-19 outpatients, reducing hospital capacity strain and promoting at-home care. The device continuously tracks body temperature, heart rate, oxygen saturation, and motion, allowing patients to receive real-time health updates while performing daily activities.

By leveraging electrical device theory, sensor theory, and signal conditioning, this solution ensures early warning alerts when vitals deviate from safe thresholds, enabling timely intervention and preventing critical complications.

How?

  • 3D CAD Design: Created precise SOLIDWORKS models for the device housing, including soldering enclosures, button interfaces, hinges, and cable routing.
  • Sensor Integration: Utilized a SparkFun Pulse Oximeter and Heart Rate Sensor, an M5STACK NCIR Temperature Sensor, and an accelerometer for motion detection to enhance measurement accuracy.
  • Electronics & Programming: Used Arduino for sensor interfacing and Python for data processing, ensuring reliable real time monitoring and analysis.
  • Applied low-pass Butterworth filtering in Python to reduce noise in EMG and temperature signals.
  • Design Optimization: Conducted Design of Experiments (DOE) in Minitab to refine sensor placement and enclosure design, improving signal quality and user comfort.
  • Quality Assurance: Performed failure analysis, root cause analysis, and correction methods to enhance system durability and performance.
  • Project Management: Managed project budgets, delivered weekly progress reports, and ensured adherence to technical engineering standards.

Results

The final C-Wrist device provides continuous, real-time monitoring of body temperature, heart rate, oxygen saturation, and motion, displayed on an integrated LCD screen. Patients receive instant notifications if vitals enter dangerous zones, allowing them to seek timely medical attention while continuing everyday activities.

This innovative solution not only enhances patient safety but also alleviates hospital burdens, ensuring high quality care without compromising healthcare resources.

Tech Stack: SOLIDWORKS, Arduino, Python
