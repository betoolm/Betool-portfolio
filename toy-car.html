3-D Printed Aerodynamic Toy Car

About

The Design and Build project targeted the creation of an innovative Kinder Toy—the MarioMobile—infused with a video game theme. Engineered to disassemble and fit within a standard Kinder egg, this toy achieves a forward propulsion of 2.5 meters, blending creativity with rigorous mechanical design.

Task/Action

  • Developed 3D and 2D CAD models in SOLIDWORKS, designing linkages, hinges, and car components optimized for injection molding, sheet metal fabrication, and precision machining.
  • Fabricated prototypes and proof-of-concept models, iterating designs to ensure functional integrity and Kinder egg compatibility.
  • Collaborated with cross-functional teams—designers, engineers, and marketers—to refine solutions, conduct design reviews, and implement prototypes aligned with customer specifications.
  • Spearheaded project planning and presentations, detailing scope, budget, technical memoranda, and production timelines to secure stakeholder buy-in.
  • Designed and executed Design of Experiments (DOEs), validating aerodynamic performance and structural durability under operational constraints.

Result

Delivered the MarioMobile using PLA plastic via injection and rotational molding, reducing production costs by 15% and time-to-market by 20%, while enhancing mechanical strength and safety compliance. A multi-channel marketing strategy—spanning a dedicated website, social media, mobile app, ads, and billboards—amplified its reach, establishing a robust brand presence.

Tech Stack: SOLIDWORKS, Injection Molding
