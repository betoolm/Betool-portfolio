3-D Printed Aerodynamic Toy Car

About

This project aimed to design an innovative, self-propelled toy capable of fitting inside a Kinder Egg when disassembled and driving 2.5 meters forward upon assembly. The challenge demanded precision engineering, compact design, and seamless functionality within extreme spatial constraints.

How?

  • Risk Assessment: Conducted Failure Mode and Effects Analysis (FMEA) to identify and mitigate potential design risks, such as drivetrain binding and spring failure.
  • 3D Modeling: Designed over 20 precision parts using SOLIDWORKS, ensuring all components fit within the Kinder Egg’s 39 mm x 28 mm capsule when disassembled.
  • Tolerance Stack Up Analysis: Employed SOLIDWORKS DimXpert and Excel-based stack-up calculations to evaluate cumulative tolerances, ensuring proper part fit and seamless assembly
  • Fatigue Analysis: Simulated cyclic loading to ensure long-term durability of the drivetrain and spring mechanism across multiple wind-and-release cycles.
  • Design for Manufacturability (DFM): Applied SOLIDWORKS DFMXpress to optimize parts for 3D printing and added draft angles.
  • Dimensional Validation: Used digital calipers to measure printed parts, ensuring adherence to GD&T specifications within ±0.1 mm tolerance.
  • Functional Testing: Conducted multiple wind-and-release trials, ensuring consistent travel distance and drivetrain reliability.

Results

Fully functional toy, capable of driving 2.5 meters forward, with smooth, consistent performance under multiple winda and release cycles.

Compact, user friendly design, fitting perfectly into the Kinder Egg capsule when disassembled while ensuring intuitive assembly.

Tech Stack: SOLIDWORKS, Injection Molding
