Water Debris Collector

About

The Yangtze River, one of the world’s most polluted waterways in China, presents severe environmental threats due to debris accumulation, endangering marine ecosystems, human health, and climate stability. This project aimed to design an innovative water debris collector, assessing its economic feasibility and impact through cost-benefit analysis over its lifecycle. Key criteria included minimizing marine life disruption, using accessible materials, ensuring minimal vessel obstruction, and enabling efficient manufacturability.

Task/Action

  • Led the design of a water debris collector in SOLIDWORKS, integrating sheet metal electronics enclosures, weight sensor systems, and modular components for scalable deployment.
  • Executed design studies—tolerance analysis, root-cause investigations, and project presentations—to validate system robustness and stakeholder alignment.
  • Engineered a water filtration system 30% more efficient than baseline designs, optimizing flow dynamics and debris capture rates.
  • Fabricated cardboard and plastic prototypes, refining layouts to enhance machine manufacturability and operational efficacy.
  • Designed and conducted Design of Experiments (DOEs), down-selecting configurations to maximize performance under variable river conditions.

Result

Developed the Bank Filter with Guiding Vehicle system using SOLIDWORKS and 3D CAD modeling, featuring autonomous guiding vehicles, camera sensors, and bank filters. Camera arrays detect debris across the river’s width, signaling vehicles to redirect waste to filters, extracting debris while returning purified water. The design balances high initial costs with reduced long-term maintenance, achieving a sustainable solution for debris mitigation with minimal ecological footprint.

Tech Stack: SOLIDWORKS, 3D CAD, DOE Optimization
