Vehicle Gear and Holder Design

About

The goal was to develop and refine an engineering design solution through iterative design, simulation, and prototyping, ensuring a robust, cost effective product ready for real world application in a snowmobile.

How?

  • Created a Bill of Materials (BOM) and part numbering system for seamless procurement and manufacturing.

  • 3D Modeling: Designed over 100 custom parts in SolidWorks, including complex geometries, assemblies, and subassemblies, ensuring seamless integration across the blade system.

  • 2D Technical Drawings: Generated manufacturing ready drawings with GD&T to define critical features, ensuring precise fit and function:

    • Form Tolerances: Flatness and straightness applied to blade edges for smooth cutting performance.
    • Orientation Tolerances: Perpendicularity ensured alignment between the blade and housing.
    • Location Tolerances: True position applied to holes and fastener locations for precise assembly.

  • Tolerance Stack Up Analysis: Used SolidWorks DimXpert and Excel based stack up calculations to evaluate cumulative tolerances across mating parts, ensuring perfect assembly fit without interference.

  • Assembly Modeling: Created fully constrained assemblies to simulate real world movement and stress points, validating fit and functionality.

  • Static Load Simulation: Conducted stress and displacement analysis in SolidWorks Simulation to identify critical stress points and refine part geometry for enhanced strength without unnecessary material use.

  • Motion Analysis: Simulated blade movement and system dynamics to evaluate kinematic performance, ensuring smooth operation under expected loading conditions.

  • Fatigue Analysis: Assessed design durability under cyclic loading conditions to predict lifespan and reliability.

  • DFM: Applied SolidWorks DFMXpress to identify features that could complicate machining, simplifying complex geometries while maintaining functionality:

    • Reduced unnecessary tight tolerances on non critical surfaces.
    • Standardized hole sizes for efficient drilling operations.

  • Material Selection: Recommended 6061-T6 aluminum for structural components, balancing strength, machinability, and corrosion resistance.

    • Result

    Achieved a fully functional blade system with 99.9% dimensional accuracy praised as “one of the most accurate designs” by instructional assistants delivering unmatched reliability in harsh snowmobile environments.

    Tech Stack: SolidWorks, FEA Simulation, DFM Principles
