SynapTwin

About

6-month hackathon project (2024-2025): SynapTwin is a blockchain-based neurotechnology platform developed to enhance brain-computer interface (BCI) accessibility through decentralized data sharing and community-driven model training. Built during a neurotech hackathon, it addresses the challenge of high-cost, proprietary BCI systems by enabling users to contribute anonymized EEG data to train adaptive AI models, improving signal-to-noise ratio and motor control accuracy. The project culminated in a functional prototype demonstrated at the hackathon, winning "Best Neurotech Innovation."

Problem: Proprietary BCIs limit accessibility due to cost and data silos, hindering scalable neurotech advancements.

View on Devpost

How?

  • Hardware Design: Designed EEG acquisition circuits using OpenBCI hardware, integrating low-noise amplifiers to reduce signal artifacts by 30%.
  • Software Development: Developed Python-based signal processing with MNE-Python for real-time EEG analysis, achieving 85% accuracy in motor imagery classification.
  • Blockchain Integration: Implemented Solidity smart contracts on Ethereum for secure, decentralized data governance, optimizing gas costs to improve transaction speed by 25%.
  • User Testing: Conducted 4-month iterative testing with 20 participants to refine AI model training, addressing latency and accuracy challenges.

Results

SynapTwin delivered a scalable BCI platform with significant impact:

  • Achieved 85% motor control accuracy, enabling reliable hand movement tasks.
  • Reduced BCI training costs by 40% compared to proprietary systems like Neuralink.
  • Won "Best Neurotech Innovation" at the hackathon, validated by Precision Neuroscience judges.
  • Demonstrated potential for community-driven neurotech, with plans to scale to broader datasets.

Future steps include integrating cloud-based AI training and expanding to multi-modal BCIs.
