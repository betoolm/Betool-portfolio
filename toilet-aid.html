Toilet Aid

About

The Toilet Aid was designed to improve independence, hygiene, and accessibility for individuals with limited dexterity, mobility impairments, arthritis, or post-surgical recovery needs. The goal was to develop a low-cost, easy-to-use, and hygienic wiping aid that allows users to handle toilet paper independently. The design prioritized affordability (<$10 CAD), ease of use, hygiene, and ergonomic effectiveness, ensuring it can be widely adopted by elderly individuals, people with disabilities, and underserved communities. The device also complies with ISO 17966:2016, the standard for assistive devices for personal hygiene, ensuring safety, performance, and ease of use.

How?

  • User Centered Design Approach: Conducted client validation meetings and consultations to refine design requirements based on grip strength, range of motion, and hygiene needs.
  • Material Selection & Cost Optimization: Identified kitchen tongs ($2-$6 CAD) as a readily available, cost effective base component.

Design Enhancements:

  • Custom Handle Grip: Designed to improve ergonomics for users with reduced hand strength, ensuring a comfortable and firm grasp.
  • Adjustable Reach: Allows customization for various mobility needs, supporting users with shoulder, wrist, and finger limitations.
  • Locking Mechanism: Integrated to secure toilet paper during use, preventing accidental slippage.
  • Mechanical & Force Calculations: Maximum grip strength required: 25 N (as per ISO 17966:2016) was achieved.
  • Lever arm calculations ensured that users could apply sufficient force with minimal hand strength, reducing fatigue and maximizing efficiency.
  • Evaluated contact pressure and force distribution across the wiping surface to ensure sufficient pressure for proper hygiene.

Finite Element Analysis (FEA):

  • Conducted static stress, strain, and displacement simulations using ANSYS to validate structural integrity.
  • Confirmed that the design withstands multiple use cycles without mechanical failure.
  • Analyzed material deformation under repeated applied forces to ensure durability.
  • Gathered user feedback on ease of use, hygiene, and effectiveness, comparing various assistive tools like dish scrubbers, hangers, and toilet brushes.

Results

The Toilet Aid successfully met cost, hygiene, usability, and ISO compliance criteria, offering a simple, ergonomic, and widely accessible solution:

The final design maintained a low cost ($2-$6 CAD) while ensuring long-term usability and durability.

ISO 17966:2016 Compliance: Ensured the safety, performance, and ease of use required for assistive personal hygiene devices.

Ergonomic & Functional Design: Users can operate the device with one or two hands, ensuring sufficient grip strength and wiping pressure.

Mechanical Validation: Calculations confirmed that the device meets the 25 N force requirement with a factor of safety (FOS) > 2, ensuring reliability under normal use.

User Validation: Surveys confirmed high ease-of-use ratings, with improved cleaning efficiency compared to alternative assistive devices.
